Legal Architecture Stack
Asset → Legal Wrapper → Rights Model → Token Standard → Compliance → Transfer Venue → Monetization
SEC: token ≠ asset
SEC Position (2023–2025): Tokenized securities are still securities. Tokenization does not change the underlying legal obligations, registration requirements, or investor protections. A token is the on-chain representation of rights that must already exist in valid off-chain legal documents. The asset, the legal wrapper, and the rights model must be established first — the token standard is layer 4 of 7, not layer 1.

The Critical Principle
The token does not create the right. The right must exist first — in a signed operating agreement, a recorded deed of trust, a promissory note, or an SEC-filed Form D. The smart contract automates the administration of those rights: transferring tokens, distributing USDC, enforcing whitelist gates. But if the off-chain documents are missing or defective, no amount of on-chain logic makes the token legitimate or the offering legal.
Layers 1–3 are established by attorneys, not developers.
Layers 4–5 are implemented in Solidity.
Layers 6–7 are configured per deal and per product.

How the Accumulator Works
STEP 1 — MANAGER DEPOSITS
Manager calls depositDistribution(amount, dealRef) after each quarterly close. USDC is pulled into the contract.
STEP 2 — ACCUMULATOR UPDATES
accumulatedPerToken += amount / circulatingInvestorTokens. No loops — O(1) gas regardless of investor count.
STEP 3 — INVESTOR CLAIMS
Each investor calls claimRewards() to pull their share. Pending rewards are preserved through transfers via pendingSettled.
